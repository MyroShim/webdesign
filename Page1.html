Retro Gaming

Retro gaming, a beloved pastime cherished by enthusiasts worldwide, harkens back to the golden era of video games before the turn of the millennium. From iconic arcade cabinets like Pac-Man and Space Invaders to the revolutionary home consoles such as the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) and Sega Genesis, retro gaming encompasses a rich tapestry of immersive experiences and timeless classics. The pixelated adventures of Super Mario Bros., the adrenaline-fueled races of Sonic the Hedgehog, and the epic battles of The Legend of Zelda captivated generations of gamers and laid the foundation for modern gaming as we know it. With their simple yet addictive gameplay mechanics and vibrant 8-bit graphics, retro games continue to evoke nostalgia and captivate players of all ages, serving as a testament to the enduring legacy of gaming's formative years.

The Legend of Zelda

There is a lot of Zelda chatter doing the rounds right now, with the long-awaited and much-hyped The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild 2 being the topic of much discussion, and while we're obsessing over the new Zelda game, many of us may want to check out some of the older ones, of which there are a great many.

If you do decide to trek through Zelda history, you will find that there are loads of games to get through, and the easiest way to do it is to play them in release order. But what if you want to experience the story in chronological order? Well, that is where things get a lot more complicated. Here weve put together two lists for both release and chronological order of this great franchise, starting with release order:

  • The Legend of Zelda (1986)
  • Zelda II: The Adventure of Link (1987)
  • The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past (1991)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Link’s Awakening (1993)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Majora’s Mask (2000)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Seasons and Oracle of Ages (2001)
  • The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker (2002)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Four Swords Adventures (2004)
  • The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap (2004)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess (2006)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass (2007)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Spirit Tracks (2009)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword (2011)
  • The Legend of Zelda: A Link Between Worlds (2013)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Tri Force Heroes (2015)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (2017)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom (2023)

It's worth noting that Hyrule Warriors: Age of Calamity (2020), Cadence of Hyrule (2019) and numerous other titles are considered spinoffs rather than core entries, which is why we've left them off this list. You can normally spot a spinoff by the fact that Nintendo isn't the only developer that worked on it. Let's be honest, following a narrative with the Zelda games is by no means straightforward and there are many different variations of what people think to be the go-to order for experiencing the lengthy saga. It gets complicated thanks to the three, at least, different timelines that the games exist in the games. And because of that, there is no definitive order that you can play them in that everyone will agree on. The main timelines that you need to know about are the Child, Adult and Fallen Hero timelines. There are also some games which come before the split into different timelines, which here we've decided to call the Prequel Timeline just so we have something to call it. So, with that in mind, this is just our preferred way of working through the games if you want the story to flow the best it can. Take a look below for our recommended chronological order:

  • The Legend of Zelda: Skyward Sword (2011) (Prequel)
  • The Legend of Zelda: The Minish Cap (2004) (Prequel)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Four Swords (2002) (Prequel)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998) (Child & Adult)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Majora’s Mask (2000) (Child)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess (2006) (Child)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Four Swords Adventures (2004) (Child)
  • The Legend of Zelda: The Wind Waker (2002) (Adult)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass (2007) (Adult)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Spirit Tracks (2009) (Adult)
  • The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past (1991) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Seasons and Oracle of Ages (2001) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Link’s Awakening (1993) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda: A Link Between Worlds (2013) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Tri Force Heroes (2015) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda (1986) (Fallen Hero)
  • Zelda II: The Adventure of Link (1987) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild (2017) (Fallen Hero)
  • The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom (2023) (Fallen Hero)
